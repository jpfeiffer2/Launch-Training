When Prevention Is Not Enough
Day 1 focused on preventing unsafe situations. Day 2 focuses on what happens when an incident still occurs—and how your response protects everyone around it.
You may not be able to prevent every injury—but your response can prevent the situation from becoming worse.
1

Stay Calm

Panic creates confusion. A controlled response begins with the monitor remaining composed.

2

Protect the Area

Stop nearby activity and prevent the incident from becoming a second injury.

3

Get the Right Help

Notify a Supervisor or Manager and follow the correct response procedure.

Injury Response
Injuries Still Happen
When an injury happens, your first job is scene control.
1. Stop playWhistle. Stop nearby activity.
2. Clear the trampoline areaInjured trampoline + all adjacent trampolines, including diagonals.
3. Move guests backKeep guests in a safe waiting area.
4. Call leadershipGet a Supervisor or Manager immediately.
Key Rule
Do not let one injury become two.
Protect the scene. Get the right help.
Injury Response
Role Boundary
If You Are Not Certified
You are still important in an injury response. Your job is to control the scene, communicate clearly, and support leadership.
Key StandardDo not become the medical responder beyond your training.
✓

Do

  • Stop play near the injured guest.
  • Clear guests back from the area.
  • Call a Supervisor or Manager.
  • Hand off the First Aid bag when leadership arrives.
  • Follow leadership direction.
×

Do Not

  • Diagnose the injury.
  • Move the injured guest unless directed.
  • Provide care you are not trained to give.
  • Leave the attraction uncontrolled.
  • Let guests crowd the area.
You are responsible for control, communication, and support — not medical treatment beyond your training.
