Readiness

Arrives in proper Launch uniform and is ready for floor training
Has required whistle, radio/communication method, and First Aid bag as assigned
Reviews required First Aid bag contents with trainer

Guest-Ready Area Standards

Checks assigned attraction area for trash, debris, loose items, and trip hazards
Checks padding, netting, court surface, equipment, and entry/exit areas for visible concerns
Identifies conditions that should be corrected before guests continue using the area

Unsafe Condition Response

Explains when play should be stopped due to an unsafe condition
Keeps guests away from damaged equipment or unsafe areas
Calls leadership and waits for clearance before reopening the attraction or affected area

Injury Response

Takes all injury reports seriously, including minor-looking injuries
Stops nearby play and clears space around the injured guest
Calls leadership and gives the exact location
Explains that injured guests should not be moved unless directed by leadership or emergency responders

Code Blue

Identifies Code Blue signs, including serious injury or medical emergency indicators
Explains why Code Blue is used instead of describing serious injuries over the radio
Demonstrates a clear Code Blue call using the exact location

Role Boundaries

Explains the monitor’s role: control the scene, communicate, and support leadership
Explains that medical care should only be provided by trained/certified/authorized team members
Hands off the First Aid bag and follows leadership direction during an injury-response scenario

Bodily Fluids / Sanitation

Treats blood, vomit, or other bodily fluids as a closure issue
Stops play and keeps guests away from the affected area
Notifies leadership and waits for cleaning, sanitation, and clearance before reopening
Explains that cleanup should only happen when directed and properly protected

Floor Training Completion

Completes Day 2 floor-training review with trainer
Trainer confirms trainee is ready to return to the Day 2 exam
