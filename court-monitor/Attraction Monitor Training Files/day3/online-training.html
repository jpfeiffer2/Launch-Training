Coverage may change

Your Assignment May Change

Some shifts may assign you to one attraction. Other shifts may require you to cover multiple attractions or a larger zone.

The standard stays the same

  • Stay active and keep scanning.
  • Move with purpose.
  • Correct unsafe behavior anywhere in your zone.

Guest engagement

Safety First, Fun Always

Strong Attraction Monitors do more than correct problems. When the area is under control, they help guests have fun without lowering the safety standard.

The rule that always comes first

  • Keep scanning before, during, and after guest interaction.
  • Engage only when you can still watch the area safely.
  • Fun never replaces the rules.

Vigilance
Complacency risk

Slow Days Are Not Easy Mode

A quiet floor still requires active monitoring.

Purpose

Address the risk that monitors become less alert when guest volume is low.

  • Unsafe behavior can still happen with only a few guests.
  • Guests may spread out, making coverage harder.
  • Equipment issues or hazards can appear at any time.

Safety Checks
Before the park opens

Daily Safety Checks

Daily Safety Checks are completed and logged before guests use the attractions.

Purpose

Provide a brief overview of daily checks while clarifying that they are generally completed by experienced monitors, supervisors, or leadership.

  • Checks help confirm attractions are safe and guest ready before opening.
  • They may include padding, netting, courts, equipment, entry/exit areas, and visible hazards.
  • New monitors should understand why checks matter even if they are not assigned to complete the official log.

Ongoing Safety
After opening

Safety Checks Do Not Replace Active Monitoring

The opening check starts the day. Active monitoring protects the day.

Purpose

Prevent the mindset that a morning safety check means conditions cannot change later.

  • Padding can shift, equipment can become damaged, and guests can create hazards.
  • Trash, spills, or bodily fluids can appear during the shift.
  • Report anything unsafe you notice, even if the attraction passed the opening check.

When You Find a Problem Mid-Shift

Connect Day 3 floor coverage back to the Day 2 response standard.

Purpose

Reinforce what to do when a safety issue appears after the park has already opened.

  • Stop play if the area is unsafe.
  • Keep guests away and call leadership.
  • Support the response and wait for clearance before reopening.

Rule Enforcement
Repeat behavior

Repeat Rule Breakers

Repeated unsafe behavior needs escalation, not endless warnings.

Purpose

Teach judgment around guests who continue unsafe behavior after correction.

  • One correction may be education; repeated behavior becomes a pattern.
  • Call leadership when a guest continues unsafe behavior.
  • Do not physically remove a guest yourself.

Guest Escalation
Stay calm

Upset Guests or Parents

An upset guest should not cause the monitor to lose control of the attraction or zone.

Purpose

Keep the original angry-parent/escalation concept and make it specific to attraction monitoring.

  • Stay calm and professional.
  • Briefly explain the safety reason without arguing.
  • Call leadership early and keep monitoring your assigned area.

Documentation
Details matter

Incident Reports and Details

Accurate details help leadership understand what happened after the moment is over.

Purpose

Preserve incident-report awareness without re-teaching the full injury-response process.

  • You may be asked for names, time, location, attraction, and what you observed.
  • Do not guess, exaggerate, or fill in details you did not see.
  • Report what you saw, heard, or were told.

Area Ownership
Clean, reset, and report

End-of-Shift Cleaning and Area Reset

A strong monitor helps maintain the area, not just watch it.

Purpose

Make it clear that Attraction Monitors are responsible for leaving their assigned area clean, reset, and ready for the next shift or next business day.

  • Wipe down pads and equipment in the assigned area.
  • Vacuum floors and remove trash, loose items, and debris.
  • Additional duties vary by park and may include restrooms, seating areas, trash cans, tables, benches, or other guest areas.
  • Follow local closing duties and report damaged or unsafe items instead of just cleaning around them.

Final Floor Readiness Challenge

Apply the full Day 3 mindset to a realistic floor-coverage scenario.

Purpose

Use a final scenario to test multi-area awareness, prioritization, communication, and escalation.
